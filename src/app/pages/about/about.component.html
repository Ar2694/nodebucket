Theodor Seuss Geisel, known as Dr. Seuss, is a writer and a cartoonist who published more than 60 books. His bestseller books were the Cat in the Hat and Green Eggs and Ham, which contain rhymes and characters that made it famous. Geisel was born on March 2, 1904, in Springfield, Massachusett. He lived with his parents until he turned 18 and left home to attend Dartmouth College, where he became the editor in chief of its humor magazine, Jack-O-Lantern. After graduating from Dartmouth, he attended the University of Oxford in England and then later dropped out of Oxford in 1927. After returning to America, he decided to pursue cartooning full time and published numerous magazines. He wrote many books in a new, simplified vocabulary style throughout his career and using his older, more elaborate technique. Geisel published more than 60 books, including well-known books like ‘How the Grinch Stole Christmas,’ ‘The Cat in the Hat,’ and ‘Green Eggs and Ham’. Several books have been transformed into full-length movies, both during his lifetime and posthumously. Geisel helps famous cartoonist Chuck Jone in making the film called ‘The Grinch Who Stole Christmas.’ Geisel won numerous awards for his work, including an Academy Award, three Emmys, and three Grammys.
